About Me

I consider myself a lifelong learner. At a young age, my parents ignited my desire for knowledge by reading to me at bedtime and encouraging me to write my own stories. I fantasized about attending college as a child, so when I had to drop out, due to finances, my whole world ended. Little did I know, this was the best thing to ever happen to me. During my gap year, I discovered Information Technology. I pursued an Associate's degree in Computer Information Systems at Bunker Hill Community College and joined Hack Diversity's inaugral cohort. Additionally, I interned at Federal Home Loan Bank of Boston and HubSpot as a Help Desk Technician. While at HubSpot, I learned about software development and grew passionate about improving the user experience. Subsequently, I enrolled in Resilient Coders, a non-profit coding bootcamp for young people of color. Because of Resilient Coders, I am now a Software Developer at Hi Marley. On the side, I write blog posts for Skillist, sit on Hack Diversity's alumni board, develop features for Dough, strategize for G| Code House, and study Computer Science at Boston University. I'm involved in all these programs because I am dedicated to pursuing my education AND educating my own community. I aim to transform my community and grant them access to financial equity through technology.
